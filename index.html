Frequently Asked Questions

What is CookE?

CookE is an AI-powered cooking companion app that generates personalized recipes on demand. Unlike traditional recipe databases, CookE creates recipes from any input you give it — upload a food photo, paste a TikTok or Instagram link, share a URL from a food blog, or just describe what you're in the mood for. Every recipe includes a full ingredient list with quantities, step-by-step instructions, prep time, and cooking tips. CookE is available on Android with iOS coming soon.

How does CookE generate recipes?

CookE uses AI to identify dishes and generate complete recipes. When you upload a photo, CookE first runs it through an on-device food classifier, then uses AI to identify the exact dish and produce a structured recipe. When you paste a link from TikTok, Instagram, or a food blog, CookE reads the content and converts it into a clean, organized recipe with ingredients, steps, and timing. Text descriptions work the same way — just type what you want and CookE creates the recipe from scratch.

Can I modify a recipe while cooking?

Yes. CookE includes a follow-up chat feature that lets you adjust recipes on the fly. If you realize you're out of an ingredient, just type "I don't have cream, what can I use?" and CookE updates the recipe instantly. You can also ask it to make the dish spicier, reduce servings, or accommodate dietary restrictions — all without starting over or searching for a different recipe. Your progress stays intact.

Is CookE free to try?

CookE offers a 7-day free trial with full access to all features. After the trial, you can choose a monthly or annual plan to continue. The annual plan saves 33% compared to monthly billing. You can cancel anytime during the trial and won't be charged.

Can I import recipes from TikTok or Instagram?

Yes. Paste any TikTok, Instagram Reel, or recipe URL directly into CookE and it will read the content, identify the dish, and convert it into a clear, structured recipe with organized ingredients and step-by-step instructions. You can also upload photos of handwritten recipe cards or cookbook pages — CookE reads and structures them the same way.
